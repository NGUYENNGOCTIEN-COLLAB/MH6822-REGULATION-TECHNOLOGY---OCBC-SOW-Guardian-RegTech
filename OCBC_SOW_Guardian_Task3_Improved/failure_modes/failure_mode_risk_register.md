# Failure-Mode Risk Register

| Failure mode | Cause | Impact | Likelihood | Severity | Control | Residual risk | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rule changes mid-period | Rule engine not updated or open cases not re-run | Cases assessed under obsolete requirements | Medium | High | Versioned rules, effective dates, rule-diff alert, open-case impact analysis | Some legal interpretation uncertainty remains | Compliance rule owner |
| Model drift | AI behaviour, typologies, or document formats change | More hallucinated or unsupported source-of-wealth claims | Medium | High | Drift thresholds, unsupported-claim monitoring, model-risk review | Drift may be detected after some weak cases pass | Model Risk Manager |
| Human rubber-stamping | Reviewers approve AI drafts without challenge | Human-in-the-loop becomes symbolic | High | High | Human Challenge Log, reviewer-pattern monitoring, escalation for low challenge rates | Reviewers may still game metrics | Financial Crime Compliance Head |
| Jurisdictional misconfiguration | Wrong booking location or cross-border flag selected | Wrong rule set applied | Medium | High | Booking-location check, branch/agency check, dual-rule activation, manual override approval | Complex cases may still need legal review | Compliance Operations |
| Contradictory jurisdictional requirements | US and Singapore rules create different control expectations | Confusion over whether stricter or lighter controls apply | Medium | High | No-silent-downgrade rule and additive controls for cross-border cases | Business may object to slower review | AI Governance Committee |
| Privacy/data-security failure | Sensitive documents processed or retained improperly | Customer privacy harm and regulatory exposure | Low/Medium | Very High | Secure vault, role-based access, access logs, retention controls, no uncontrolled model training | Insider misuse risk remains | Technology Risk / Data Protection Officer |
| False negative | Illicit or unsupported wealth accepted as legitimate | Financial crime risk enters the banking system | Medium | Very High | Red friction state, senior compliance approval, contradiction detection, post-review escalation monitoring | Some sophisticated financial crime may remain hidden | Financial Crime Compliance |
| False positive | Legitimate customer incorrectly flagged | Delayed onboarding, intrusive document requests, relationship harm | Medium | Medium | Explainable escalation reason, proportional follow-up, appeal/review path | Customer friction cannot be eliminated | Relationship Manager and Compliance Officer |
| Threshold miscalibration | Thresholds tuned for efficiency rather than risk | Too many weak cases pass or too many good cases escalate | Medium | High | Periodic threshold review, sensitivity testing, outcome monitoring | Calibration limited by lack of real production data | Model Risk Manager |
| Vendor interface design failure | Dashboard hides uncertainty or over-emphasizes green status | Users gain false confidence | Medium | High | Uncertainty labels, evidence gaps, no simple compliance badge, audit logs | Vendor-client incentives may still create pressure | Soteria Product Owner |
